what is climate change?


Climate change is a collective problem that requires collective action. We owe it to ourselves, our children, and future generations to take responsibility for our actions.
Rising temperatures, melting ice caps, and intensifying natural disasters are not just statistics; they're realities that demand immediate action. Climate change is:
- Displacing communities and destroying homes
- Devastating ecosystems and biodiversity
- Threatening food security and economic stability



Science of the climate change

Human activities are causing climate change. We're releasing catastrophic levels of greenhouse gases, and the consequences are dire. But we also have the knowledge, technology, and collective will to transition to a sustainable future. Climate change is caused by human activities that release greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat, leading to rising temperatures, more extreme weather events, and devastating consequences.

The Consequences of climate change

Rising sea levels threaten coastal communities and islands. Intensifying hurricanes, wildfires, and droughts destroy homes and livelihoods. Melting glaciers and ice sheets jeopardize our freshwater supply.

we must do for enviornment


1. Reduce, reuse, recycle
2. Use public transport or electric vehicles
3. Eat plant-based and support sustainable agriculture
4. Vote for climate-conscious leaders
6. Reduce energy consumption: Use energy-efficient appliances, turn off lights, and insulate your home.
7. Switch to renewable energy: Invest in solar panels or renewable energy credits.
8. Eat sustainably: Choose plant-based options, reduce food waste, and support local farmers.
9. Conserve water: Take shorter showers, fix leaks, and use water-efficient appliances.
10. Get involved: Participate in climate protests, contact your representatives, and support climate-friendly policies.
