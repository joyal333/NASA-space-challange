Increasingly Fossil Fuels:

What is it?

Fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) are non-renewable energy sources releasing carbon dioxide (CO2) when burned.


Causes:


1. Energy demand growth
2. Economic development
3. Lack of renewable energy infrastructure

Effects:


1. Greenhouse gas emissions (CO2, CH4, N2O)
2. Air pollution (particulates, ozone)
3. Water pollution (extraction, transportation)
4. Land degradation (mining, drilling)
1. Climate change acceleration
2. Human health impacts (respiratory issues, cancer)
3. Environmental degradation
4. Resource depletion

Visual Illustration:


1900: 1.5 billion barrels/year
2020: 33.7 billion barrels/year
2050: 50 billion barrels/year (projected)

Regional Examples:


1. US: 20% of global fossil fuel consumption
2. China: 25% of global fossil fuel consumption
3. EU: 15% of global fossil fuel consumption

What Can We Do?


1. Transition to renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
2. Increase energy efficiency
3. Electrify transportation and industry
4. Carbon capture and storage
5. Sustainable land-use practices

Some reliable sources for further information:


- International Energy Agency (IEA)
- National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)
- Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)
- Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
